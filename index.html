About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences (MPI MiS) in the group of Bernd Sturmfels. My research focusses on the holonomic gradient method applied to statistics. In particular, I want to frame certain hard Maximum Likelihood Estimation and Bayesian Inference problems in the context of D-modules and apply the resulting exact and fast holonomic gradient descent algorithms.

Before joining the Max Planck Institute, I did a DPhil (PhD) in Mathematics at Oxford University under the supervision of Heather Harrington, Thomas Woolley and Eamonn Gaffney. During my doctoral research I used algebraic methods to investigate chemical reaction networks with a particular focus on model testing and model selection for steady state data.

Research Interests

My research is in the intersection of applied algebra, data science and chemical reaction network theory. In particular, I develop practical algebraic methods and algorithms to analyse data coming from problems in statistics, systems biology, chemical reaction networks and Turing's theory of pattern formation.
