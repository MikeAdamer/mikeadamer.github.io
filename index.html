About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher athe the ETH Zurich in the Machine Learning and Computational Biology Group of Prof. Karsten Borgwardt. My research focusses on applied machine learning for personalised medicine. Currently I am working of time series anomaly detection and image segmentation.

Before joining the ETH I did a short postdoc at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences (MPI MiS) in the group of Bernd Sturmfels. There, I investigated the holonomic gradient method and its application to statistics. In particular, we framed certain hard Maximum Likelihood Estimation and Bayesian Inference problems in the context of D-modules and applied the resulting exact and fast holonomic gradient descent algorithms.

I obtained my DPhil (PhD) in Mathematics at Oxford University under the supervision of Heather Harrington, Thomas Woolley and Eamonn Gaffney. During my doctoral research I used algebraic methods to investigate chemical reaction networks with a particular focus on model testing and model selection for steady state data. A copy of my DPhil thesis can be found here.

Research Interests

My research is in the intersection of applied algebra, data science and biochemical systems. In particular, during my DPhil (PhD) developed practical algebraic methods and algorithms to analyse data coming from problems in statistics, systems biology, chemical reaction networks and Turing's theory of pattern formation. More recently I have become interested in data science problems arising in biomedical applications, including time series anomaly detction and image analysis.
